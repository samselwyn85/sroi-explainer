Where the value sits

Each benefit's share of the total. If one or two lines dominate, that's where your evidence needs to be strongest.

    Under the hood

    How each line is calculated

    # for each benefit, for each year t = 1, 2, 3
    quantity = cohort × (segment % ÷ 100) × (success % ÷ 100) × units per person
    value = quantity × proxy $ per unit
    pv = value ÷ (1 + rate) ^ (t − 0.5) ← mid-year discounting, CBAx-style
     
    BCR = total PV of benefits ÷ cost PV
    NPV = total PV of benefits − cost PV

    Deadweight, attribution and drop-off are not separate multipliers here — they should already be embedded in your parameters. Segment share counts only people plausibly exposed above business-as-usual; success rate is net of what would have happened anyway; the taper across Years 1–3 is your drop-off. Set a year's parameters to zero if the benefit doesn't persist (one-off, event-based outcomes).

    What this tool deliberately does not do

    It only computes the priced stream. Outcomes you've decided to hold whole — intrinsic, taonga outcomes — don't belong in here as lines with clever proxies. Report them alongside the ratio as evidenced narrative, and keep the two streams separate all the way to the decision-maker. Refreshing the page clears the model, so note down parameters you want to keep.
